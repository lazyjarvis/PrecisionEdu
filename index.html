  • Do you want your child to be a tycoon in any technology of his/her interest?

  • Do you want him/her to gain leadership skills to lead high performance teams or business?

With changes in economy and employment, it is a high time for parents/students to realize the best ways to prepare themselves to be successful in career and life. In today’s world, it is vital for students to know ways to make money work them rather than working for money.

Unfortunately, our conventional education system fails to train students in these aspects because of the gap in the curriculum.

For instance, foreign language programs that the students take fails to prepare them to speak the language fluently in most cases. Modern programs on advanced technologies like robotics fails to train students to develop robots for practical use such as agricultural or industrial work. We want them to be successful entrepreneurs. The ways to attempt risks, overcoming failures, or strategic planning are not often emphasized.

Often students are encouraged to follow their passion. It does not make sense if the passion fails to serve our practical needs to support their life in this modern materialistic world. Therefore, a purpose-based approach is essential rather than a passion-based approach. Again, IQ based approach has limitations in a way that it limits the component “happiness” from success.

Every individual has different interests, strengths, skills, and aspirations. Curriculum can only offer a broad framework. A precision education opportunity is vital to support the student with a roadmap and equip them with the specialized skillsets to meet their career goals smoothly.
